Risk Management Framework

Our comprehensive risk management framework aims to protect capital while optimizing returns through disciplined position sizing, strategic stop-loss placement, and continuous portfolio monitoring. We employ systematic risk controls across all energy trading strategies with a focus on capital preservation and controlled exposure to volatile markets.

Why Our Risk Management Approach

  • Aims to provide capital preservation, controlled exposure, and consistent risk-adjusted returns
  • Lower potential volatility through systematic position sizing
  • Actively managed by experienced risk professionals
  • Real-time monitoring and automated risk controls

Risk Management Components

Position Sizing Risk

Risks associated with determining appropriate position sizes relative to account capital. Our systematic approach ensures no single position exceeds predefined risk limits, protecting against overexposure to any single trade or market movement.

Stop-Loss and Exit Risk

Risks related to stop-loss placement and execution. Market gaps, slippage, or technical failures may prevent stop-loss orders from executing at intended prices, potentially resulting in losses greater than anticipated.

Market Volatility Risk

Energy markets can experience extreme volatility due to geopolitical events, supply disruptions, or demand shocks. Rapid price movements may exceed risk parameters and result in significant losses even with proper risk management in place.

Correlation Risk

Risks that seemingly uncorrelated positions may move together during market stress, amplifying portfolio risk beyond individual position limits. Our correlation analysis helps identify and manage these interconnected risks.

Liquidity Risk

Risks associated with the ability to enter or exit positions at desired prices. During periods of low liquidity, spreads may widen significantly, and execution may be delayed, impacting risk management effectiveness.

Leverage and Margin Risk

Risks from using leverage or margin, which can amplify both gains and losses. Margin calls may require additional capital or forced position liquidation, potentially at unfavorable prices.
